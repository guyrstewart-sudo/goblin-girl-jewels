02 · Origin Story

From the flame

Goblin Girl Jewels is the craft of Larissa Alboucrek — a flow artist and fire dancer. The same hands that spin hoops of flame gather raw stones and solder them into wearable spells.

The brand is born at the intersection of three worlds: the earth (sustainably harvested crystals and fossils), the festival (warm, wide-open, flow-arts energy), and the spirit (intention, protection, magick). That fusion is what makes a Goblin Girl piece feel alive.

06 · Name & Logo

The mark

Goblin Girl Jewels

✦ Soldered Magick ✦

The gem mark — a single faceted crystal in terracotta. Works as a favicon, a wax-seal stamp, or a hang-tag emblem on its own.

Wordmark — "Goblin Girl" in Fraunces with "Jewels" in italic Cormorant. Always pair with the tagline Soldered Magick where space allows.

Clearspace & don'ts — give the mark room equal to one facet on all sides. Don't recolor harshly, stretch the wordmark, or set it on busy low-contrast photos.

09 · Art Direction & Photography

The look

Photography

Natural daylight, real hands, real skin. Shoot pieces held up to the sun, against weathered wood, stone, and linen. Warm, sun-lit, lived-in — never a sterile studio.

Graphic layer

Over the earthy photos, keep it minimal: thin gold hairlines, generous whitespace, a soft blush wash, and the occasional ✦ or moon-phase mark. Elegant, never cluttered.

Motion

Slow, gentle drift on the background wash; soft fades on scroll. Calm and breathing, never flashy — and always honors reduced-motion.
